This is my first blog written with blogdown.

I decided to create a personal blog site to serve the following purposes:

  • Blog gives me an opportunity to test out whatever I found interesting in the realms of Machine Learning and Deep Learning. I hope to explore in both R and Python. However, I have yet to figure out how to use the reticulate package to embed a Python session in Rstudio. A bit of free writing may be included as well both as a mean to practice my writing skill and to have my sanity in-check ❄️ . It serves as a progress tracker as well.

  • Project acts as a Portfolio showcase for potential employers. Still need to think about how to structure this page.

  • Creative will be populated once I gained enough confident in uploading my guitar playing. 😜

  • Overall, the blog site aims at improving and expanding my technical skills outside my M.S. Program.

Originally, riding on the excitement of deploying the site successfully, I was planning to update the blog everyday. Then I realize that I should stop lying to myself. So currently I am planning to write a new blog post four times a week:

  • Monday blog sets up my reading plan and studying/coding plan for the week as well as self-checks on mental health.

  • Tuesday blog will be honoring the tradition of #TidyTuesday. I will explore Machine Learning Models of the tidymodel package, run some code from Tidy Modeling with R. I will probably test out some blog project on the personal website of Julia Silge and other established R developers.

  • Wednesday blog will be messing with Python. Since all of my knowledge of Python are self-taught, I will include some algorithm and data structure practices as well.

  • Saturday blog will be on whatever I want to explore, and reflect on my progress this week.

My personal blog website is built with Hugo and deployed with Netlify 😄 . To write with blogdown, I am using blogdown: Creating Websites with R Markdown by Yihui Xie, Amber Thomas, Alison Presmanes Hill. I am aware that the primary audience of this blog site will be myself. I do however still find it interesting to keep track of my progress, and re-examine myself from a “third-person” standpoint.

So this week, I will be mainly reviewing materials from my M.S. program since I am a bit behind (I found out that I really don’t like experimental design), and reading on the regression section of Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn and TensorFlow to help my school projects too.

And yes, I have decided to use as many emojis as possible in these informal blogs to compensate my seemingly lack on emotions when writing these blogs. The fine line between professionalism and casualness is at times hard to walk through for a Nonresident Alien 👽 like myself - an official title that was given to me by USCIS.

Slow, steady progress is better than daily excuses.
